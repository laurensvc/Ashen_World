# Ember Sigils

Ember Sigils treats every card face as the frozen trace of a violent computational weather system: flow, fracture, and heat gradients locked into one emblematic moment. The algorithm does not decorate mechanics; it encodes intent. Attack cards read as rupture vectors, defense cards as pressure barriers, utility cards as redirected current, and status cards as unstable contamination fields.

The visual language is built as one-card-one-generator. Each card id owns a distinct renderer with its own motifs, field math, and deterministic seed stream. This is a meticulously crafted algorithmic system where identity is structural, not superficial: cards must differ by silhouette energy, dominant geometry, and value distribution before color is even considered.

Color remains a controlled high-contrast ritual: volcanic darks, ember oranges, forged golds, toxic greens, and sudden electric cool accents. Determinism is strict and reproducible, yet no two card IDs should collapse into the same compositional cadence. The balance between order and violence should read as painstaking optimization and master-level implementation.

Composition emphasizes clarity under combat pressure. Every generator reserves central text-safe zones while pushing motif-specific force into edges and diagonals. The result should feel like the product of countless tuning passes by a top-tier generative artist: sharp, legible, unmistakably authored, and clearly distinct card by card.
